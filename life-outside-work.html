Beyond Work

When I'm not working, I enjoy digital art, painting, dance, martial arts, and hiking—things that help me stay grounded and energized.

See more

Painting

I began painting during the COVID-19 pandemic, and I often wish I had started earlier. I gravitated toward watercolor portraits, especially of friends and family, and began painting portraits as birthday gifts. I've also experimented with acrylics and created a piece inspired by an IKEA painting of a deer, for my brother's bedroom. It still hangs in his room and remains one of my proudest creations.

Digital art

I recently started exploring digital art after my husband gifted me an iPad, and it quickly became a creative space that fits into a busy life. Unlike traditional painting, digital art allows me to create without setting up an entire workspace, which makes it easier to stay consistent. I enjoy experimenting with color through food illustrations, landscapes inspired by YouTube tutorials, and personal pieces—like artwork I created for my mother-in-law. Next, I'm excited to explore animation as a new way to bring digital art to life.

Dance

My love for dance began in childhood, imitating Bollywood dance I watched on television. Performing has always made me feel free and deeply connected to myself. Much of my confidence was shaped by my mother, who encouraged me to participate and perform from a young age at social and cultural gatherings. One day, I hope to formally train in Indian classical dance as well as a traditional Western dance form. Most of the photos I have are from my Sangeet, and they capture just how meaningful dance is to me.

Martial arts

My first exposure to martial arts began in second grade through taekwondo training. Although I was progressing well, an early competition experience left me discouraged, and I stepped away believing it wasn't for me. Years later, life came full circle when I met my husband, a black belt in taekwondo and hapkido, who encouraged me to begin training again in 2024.

Despite facing a meniscus tear that required surgery and temporarily limited my practice, returning to martial arts reminded me why I was drawn to it in the first place. Beyond self-defense, it has taught me perseverance—the discipline to keep showing up, even after setbacks. Earning my yellow belt remains a personal goal, and continuing that journey is something I look forward to.

Hiking

I'm still deciding whether I'm a beach person or a mountain person. But if I'm honest, I've always been drawn to the mountains. Hiking challenges me physically and mentally while keeping me grounded and close to nature. During my time in Hawaii, I explored nearly every major trail on Oahu. Mount Olomana was my favorite that had equal parts challenging and intimidating, and finishing it remains one of my most rewarding experiences. Reaching the summit of Koko Crater Railway Trail was another hike I was proud to complete even if the final stretch involved a fair amount of crawling.
