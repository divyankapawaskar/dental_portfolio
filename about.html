About Me

My journey into healthcare began in India, where I trained as a dentist in a busy academic clinical environment. Being immersed in that setting gave me early exposure to oral healthcare at scale. I could see how rapidly dentistry was evolving technologically, and how innovation was making individual patient care more efficient.

At the same time, I began noticing something that stayed with me. Many of the oral health challenges I observed were not individual problems—they were systemic. They reflected gaps in access, prevention, education, and infrastructure. I became increasingly curious about the systems shaping oral health outcomes across entire populations.

After moving to the United States, I chose to explore healthcare at a broader level and pursued an advanced degree in epidemiology to better understand patterns of disease, health disparities, and the socioeconomic factors that influence care long before a patient ever enters a clinic. This allowed me to study health not only at the level of individuals, but also across families, communities, and populations.

Through research and public health work, I examined healthcare systems at scale—exploring how policies, data infrastructure, and social context affect outcomes. This systems-level perspective reshaped how I think about care and reinforced my belief that dentistry is deeply connected to public health, prevention, and long-term well-being.

Living and training in the United States further clarified my goals, as I found my values aligned with a system that encourages rigor, innovation, diversity, and growth.

Today, my decision to return to dental school is intentional. What continues to draw me to dentistry is the ability to translate this systems-level understanding into direct, hands-on patient care. I want to train at the highest standard, in an environment where advanced technology, rigorous education, and innovation are deeply embedded in clinical practice. My goal is excellence—both in technical skill and in understanding how dentistry fits into a larger healthcare system. I bring with me a systems perspective shaped by public health, research, and lived experience across countries, along with a strong commitment to patient care informed by system-level understanding.
